We strive to create economic resilience through island interdependence and neighborhood economies. Healing is a centerpiece for our movement. We cannot move forward until we can heal our generational misconceptions, become trauma-informed communicators, and can rebuild a culture that is sustainable and equitable through a symbiosis with nature. We need to implement designs that eliminate economic power dynamics and therefore disable the systematic oppression that disproportionately affects minority groups and the working class. Orcas Island is a popular travel destination brimming with artistic talent, so what we accomplish here has the potential to ripple out onto the rest of the PNW. Our voices are loud. With organized efforts, thriving natural spaces, and the proper distribution of abundance, we can set the standard for a better future.

Selflessness and stewardship come more naturally once people have their needs met.

So what can we DO to make this happen?

Ironically, people who struggle the most have access to the least. Selflessness and stewardship come more naturally once people have their needs met. A good first step would be to transition unmanaged and under-utilized spaces into the hands of organizers, nature stewards, farmers, builders, and healers through community land trusts, stewardship leases, and neighborhood shared spaces. As an organization, we are working to create self-managing systems that can connect all demographics of people together.

Featured projects:

Fertile Ground

Fertile Ground is an experimental village project that aims to create affordable and low-impact housing options that increase the surrounding biodiversity for climate resilience and food security. Healing begins when basic needs are met. Housing security provides a community with the time and space to localize resources, explore education, and realize the potential of all.
